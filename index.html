Sorrento

& the Amalfi Coast

The trip begins where Italy turns mythological. Perched above the Bay of Naples with Vesuvius smoldering on the horizon, Sorrento is the essential gateway to the Amalfi Coast — lemon-scented, limestone-cliffed, and gloriously unhurried. Base here for five nights and let the coast come to you by ferry.

  • Day trip to Amalfi & Positano by private ferry from Marina Piccola
  • Pompeii — early morning (9 AM entry, out by 11 AM before the August heat peaks)
  • Ischia by hydrofoil — thermal beach parks, Castello Aragonese, family beaches
  • Lemon granita and limoncello on the Piazza Tasso at sunset
Chianti, Tuscany
Act II  ·  August 17 – 20 3 Nights  ·  Chianti, Tuscany

Tuscany

Chianti agriturismo

The road north from the coast delivers you into a different kind of beauty — ancient, unhurried, smelling of wild rosemary and sun-warmed stone. A private agriturismo in the Chianti hills, with a pool overlooking cypress-lined ridges, is the antidote to five days of sea air. Wander vineyards. Buy cheese at a sagra. Let the kids splash until dark.

  • Private villa with pool — Chianti hills between Greve and Panzano
  • Sangiovese vineyards and an estate olive grove on your doorstep
  • Farmers market in Greve in Chianti — fresh produce, local Pecorino, prosciutto
  • Day trip to Siena — Piazza del Campo, medieval backstreets, wild boar ragù

"Bologna for lunch and nothing more — a pitstop that earns its place in the legend of the perfect Italian road trip."

Venice

La Serenissima

Venice in August is hot, crowded, and completely irreplaceable. There is no city on earth like it — and Elle and Luka deserve to see it once while it still exists in something close to its original form. Stay in Dorsoduro or San Polo, away from the tourist crush, and let the city reveal itself on foot and by vaporetto.

  • Gondola through the smaller canals at golden hour — skip the Grand Canal crowds
  • Murano glass factory visit — Luka will take apart the process with questions
  • Rialto market at 7 AM — fresh seafood, raw light, the real Venice before the boats arrive
  • Cicchetti crawl through Cannaregio — small plates, prosecco, the Venetian aperitivo

Act IV  ·  August 23 – 26 3 Nights  ·  The Eternal City

Rome

The Eternal City

The finale deserves a stage equal to everything that came before it. Rome in August is blazing and magnificent — crowds thin late, fountains run cold, and the Colosseum at 8 AM belongs almost entirely to you. Three nights built around the neighborhoods the Romans actually love: Trastevere, Pigneto, Prati — and a table at Felice a Testaccio reserved the moment this brochure was written.

  • Colosseum & Circus Maximus — early entry, out before 11 AM, before the heat
  • Villa Borghese & Bioparco — a morning the kids will request on repeat for years
  • Trastevere at dusk — cobblestones, cacio e pepe, cold white wine
  • Felice a Testaccio — the cacio e pepe Melissa insisted on. Non-negotiable.

The Amalfi Drive

Go By Boat

The SS163 Amalfi Drive in August is an alternating-plate nightmare with traffic. All Amalfi Coast day trips depart by private ferry from Marina Piccola, Sorrento. Positano and Amalfi town are 30–45 minutes by sea — infinitely more scenic than the road.

Recommended: Sorrentino Boats charter
~€839–1,049 for a full-day private boat
