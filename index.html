Amalfi Coast, Campania
Act I  ·  August 12 – 17 5 Nights  ·  Campania

Sorrento

& the Amalfi Coast

Sorrento sits on a clifftop shelf above the Bay of Naples, with Vesuvius filling the horizon and ferry lines radiating to every point on the coast. The town itself is manageable — Piazza Tasso, the lemon groves, the clifftop gardens — but the real draw is using it as a launchpad. Everything on the Amalfi Coast comes to you by water.

  • Amalfi + Positano by private boat — Depart Marina Piccola. Positano in 35 min, Amalfi in 45 min. Charter via Sorrentino Boats (sorrentoinsider.com) — ~€839–1,049/day. The SS163 drive in August is gridlock on alternating plates; go by sea.
  • Pompeii — Gate 1 (Porta Marina), 9 AM timed entry. Tickets: pompeiisites.org, €16/adult, under-11 free. Leave by 11 AM. No shade, 40°C heat; hydration critical. No bags over 20L at entry.
  • Ischia day trip — SNAV hydrofoil from Porto di Sorrento, ~1 hour each way. Priority: Cavascura thermal baths, Castello Aragonese (€12/adult, under-10 free), and family beach at Sant'Angelo. Better for kids than Capri.
  • Dinner: Il Buco — Via Il Rampa Marina Piccola 5. Michelin-starred, Campanian tasting menu. Reserve well in advance. The one splurge meal of the Sorrento stay.

Tuscany

Chianti agriturismo

The drive north from the coast takes five hours on the A1 autostrada — arrive in the Chianti hills by late afternoon. Target: a private agriturismo with a pool in the Greve–Panzano corridor. This puts you 30 minutes from Siena, 45 from Florence if needed, and inside some of Italy's greatest Sangiovese country. Three nights here are the decompression between coasts.

  • The agriturismo — Book via VRBO or Airbnb; target Greve in Chianti or Panzano. Require: private pool, A/C, full kitchen, minimum 4 guests. Estates near Fontodi winery (Panzano) or Badia a Passignano (Antinori) put you inside the vineyards.
  • Greve in Chianti market — Piazza Matteotti, Saturday mornings. Pecorino Toscano DOP, cured meats, local Classico. Buy provisions for the pool. Lunch at Mangiando Mangiando on the same piazza — bistecca and house-made pici.
  • Siena day trip — 45-min drive. Piazza del Campo: free, no entry required. Torre del Mangia climb is optional (400 steps, Elle and Luka can do it). Lunch: Osteria Il Carroccio, Via del Casato di Sotto 32.
  • Fontodi winery — Località Conca d'Oro, Panzano in Chianti. Walk-in tasting, or book the estate tour. Flaccianello della Pieve is the flagship — the reason to be staying in Panzano.

Venice

La Serenissima

Stay in Dorsoduro or San Polo. Not San Marco. The difference is fifteen minutes by foot and the ability to actually hear yourself think. Water taxi from Piazzale Roma on arrival — do not attempt the vaporetto with luggage. Vaporetto Line 1 runs the Grand Canal and is the correct orientation for the first afternoon; Line 2 is faster once you know the city. Three days is enough to understand Venice; not enough to finish with it.

  • Rialto market — Pescheria (fish) + Erberia (produce), open 7 AM–noon, closed Sunday and Monday. Go Wednesday or Thursday when the fishing boats come in. All'Arco wine bar (Calle dell'Arco, San Polo) is directly adjacent — best cicchetti in the city, open from 8 AM.
  • Murano glass — Vaporetto Line 12 from Fondamente Nove, ~10 min. Barovier & Toso (Fondamenta Vetrài 28) offers factory tours. Luka will have questions about the furnaces. Elle will want a glass horse.
  • Gondola — Book direct with a gondolier, not an app. ~€80 for 30 minutes standard rate, €120 after 7 PM. Target 6 PM departure through the back canals of San Polo — not the Grand Canal.
  • Dinner: Osteria alle Testiere — Calle del Mondo Novo 5801, Castello. Only 24 seats. The best seafood in Venice. Reserve months ahead — do it now at ristoranteallestestiere.it.

Act IV  ·  August 23 – 26 3 Nights  ·  The Eternal City

Rome

The Eternal City

Three nights with no Vatican on the agenda. The city is bigger than its most visited building. Stay near Navona or in Rione Monti — both neighborhoods are central, walkable, and full of restaurants that serve Romans, not tourists. August heat peaks at 40°C by noon; the daily rhythm is early sights, long lunch, afternoon pool or nap, then late dinner after 8:30 PM.

  • Colosseum + Roman Forum + Palatine Hill — One combined ticket, €18/adult via coopculture.it. Enter at 9 AM opening, exit by 11 AM. This is not optional timing — by noon the Colosseum is a furnace. Walk 10 minutes to Circus Maximus (free) and Giardino degli Aranci on Aventine Hill for the best rooftop view of Rome.
  • Bioparco di Roma — Viale del Giardino Zoologico 1, Villa Borghese park. €18 adults, €15 kids (3–12). Luka and Elle can run free for a full morning. The park itself (Villa Borghese) is free — go in through Piazzale Flaminio.
  • Dinner: Felice a Testaccio — Via Mastro Giorgio 29. The cacio e pepe Melissa required. The standard by which every other version gets judged. Reservations: feliceatestaccio.com. August books 4–6 weeks out — reserve now.
  • Trastevere night — Da Enzo al 29 (Via dei Vascellari 29) for Roman trattoria cooking. Or Tonnarello for the neighborhood atmosphere. Both require reservations in August. Walk Piazza Santa Maria in Trastevere after dinner — the kids will remember the fountain.

The Amalfi Coast

Go By Boat

The SS163 Amalfi Drive operates on an alternating license plate system in August and runs at a crawl regardless. Do not drive it.

All Amalfi day trips depart from Marina Piccola, Sorrento, by private boat. Positano: 35 min. Amalfi town: 45 min. A boat charter gives full flexibility on timing and stops — worth every euro over the ferry schedule.
